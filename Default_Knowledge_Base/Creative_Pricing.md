# Creative Pricing Frameworks

Stop pricing by the hour. Start pricing by value.

## 1. The Value-Based Triangle
- **Cost:** What it costs you to deliver (time + software + overhead). This is your absolute floor.
- **Market:** What competitors charge. This is your reality check.
- **Value:** The ROI (Return on Investment) your work generates for the client. This is your ceiling.
*Always anchor to the Value, not the Cost.*

## 2. The 3-Tier "Goldilocks" Proposal
Never offer just one price. Always offer three options:
- **Tier 1 (The Anchor):** The basic "just get it done" option. Meets exact specs, nothing more.
- **Tier 2 (The Target):** The option you actually want them to buy. Includes strategic value adds.
- **Tier 3 (The Premium):** The "kitchen sink" option. High price, high touch. Makes Tier 2 look like a bargain.

## 3. The "Pain" Premium
If a client needs a rush job (under 48 hours), apply a 50% to 100% "rush fee" multiplier. Frame it not as a penalty, but as the cost of reprioritizing your entire schedule.
