# India Business Laws for Freelancers

A quick reference guide for independent professionals and gig workers in India (2026).

## GST (Goods and Services Tax)
- **Threshold:** Registration is mandatory if aggregate turnover exceeds ₹20 Lakhs in a financial year (₹10 Lakhs for special category states).
- **Export of Services:** If you have foreign clients (e.g., US/UK), your services are considered "Zero-Rated." You must still register for GST and obtain an LUT (Letter of Undertaking) to export without paying IGST upfront.
- **Invoicing:** Must issue tax invoices within 30 days of service provision.

## Income Tax and Presumptive Taxation (Section 44ADA)
- Freelancers in specified professions (engineering, software, design, etc.) can opt for Section 44ADA.
- Allows declaring exactly 50% of gross receipts as taxable income, bypassing the need to maintain detailed expense books.
- **Limit:** Applicable for gross receipts up to ₹75 Lakhs (updated 2024/2026 limit provided receipts in cash don't exceed 5%).

## Contracts
- Always use an NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) before discussing proprietary client ideas.
- A basic IP (Intellectual Property) assignment clause is necessary to transfer rights upon final payment. Retain rights until payment clears.
